A HUMAN RIGHTS ORGANISATION

On a mission to protect and promote human rights across the world.

The London Story

Investigate. Analyse. Protect.

The London Story was formed as an aftermath of the Indian citizenship struggle which saw one of the largest and longest-running continued social resistance in modern times. At The London Story, we strongly believe that citizenship is a tool through which majoritarian governments regularly create 'others’ who can be politically disenfranchised, dehumanized and socially reduced to margins. Our aim is to fight such dehumanization anywhere in the world by advocating for the rights of those who politically disenfranchised and are living in refugee, protracted refugee, and stateless conditions.

Objective and mission

To investigate and uncover human rights violations and abuses globally.

To analyse and expose governmental and non-governmental actions in perpetuating, supporting or ignoring the human rights violation and abuse.

To advocate for justice, peace and collective action against human rights violations.

Methods to acheive our objective

By analysis the episodes of human rights violation brought to its notice by members of its board, the media or general public.

By collecting, collating and depositing data (held confidentially) to provide a holistic understanding of the trends, patterns and sequences of human rights violations.

By engaging in seminars, talks, conferences, workshops and public lectures in order to disseminate information about human rights violations.

By raising funds for research and information dissemination through crowd-funding, donations, subscriptions and grants.

By submitting written reports, opinions, dossiers, research analysis, etc to the relevant national, international and regional bodies entrusted with implementation of human rights for all.

By engaging with the governments, political leadership, national parties and non-state stakeholders in strengthening human rights values.

By organising peaceful demonstrations, sit-ins, teach-outs and other such events to highlight the plight of victims of human rights violation and abuse.

By advocating for the rights of the victim in international, regional and national judicial forums.
